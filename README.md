## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes **employee attrition and performance** across
departments, job roles, and demographics using the IBM HR Analytics
Dataset of **1,470 employees**.

The goal is to identify key factors driving employee attrition and
evaluate performance trends to help HR teams make data-driven decisions.

---

## 🎯 Key Questions Answered
- What is the overall attrition rate?
- Which department has the highest attrition?
- Does overtime affect attrition?
- How does compensation vary across job roles?
- What factors correlate most with attrition?

## 📌 Key Findings
- 🔴 Overall attrition rate is **16.1%**
- ⏰ Overtime employees are **3x more likely** to leave **(30.5% vs 10.4%)**
- 👶 **Under 25 age group** has the highest attrition **(39.2%)**
- 💼 **Sales Representatives (39.8%)** have the highest attrition by role
- 💰 Managers earn **6.5x more (₹17,182)** than Sales Representatives **(₹2,626)**
- 📈 Performance Rating & Salary Hike strongly correlated **(0.77)**
- 🏢 **Sales department** has the highest attrition **(20.6%)**
- 💍 **Single employees (25.5%)** are most likely to leave

---

## ✅ Recommendations
- 📉 Reduce overtime especially in Sales and R&D departments
- 💰 Revisit compensation for junior roles like Sales Representatives
  and Lab Technicians
- 🌱 Create career growth paths for young employees under 25
- 🏆 Recognize high performers with better incentives and salary hikes
- 👥 Focus retention efforts on single employees and high attrition roles
